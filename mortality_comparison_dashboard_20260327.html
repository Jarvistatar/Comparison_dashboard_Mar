Key Insights
📉

Death Count Below Average

February 2026's 3 deaths is below the 2025 monthly average of 5.75/month. On volume alone, February was not an elevated month.

⚡

Dramatically Higher Case Complexity

Average LOS of 19.7 days and avg cost of ฿1.77M/case signals far more resource-intensive admissions than typical. The TTP case alone cost ฿3.69M.

🔄

Diagnosis Pattern Shift

Feb 2025: acute emergencies (sepsis, cardiac arrest, AMI). Feb 2026: complex subacute diseases (empyema, TTP, CA small bowel). No overlap in diagnosis profile year-on-year.

🏥

Departmental Shift from Cardiovascular

2025 was dominated by Cardiovascular Center (23%). Feb 2026 has zero cardiovascular deaths. Pulmonary (67%) now leads — though monthly fluctuation with n=3 is expected.

⚠

100% Respiratory Failure + AKI — Noteworthy Clustering

All 3 February 2026 deaths developed both respiratory failure and acute kidney injury. While the sample is small, this pattern of multi-organ involvement is consistent across all three distinct diagnoses (empyema, TTP, malignancy), suggesting these were late-presenting or rapidly deteriorating admissions regardless of the underlying cause.

Key YoY Observations
🔢

Volume Unchanged

Both Februaries had exactly 3 deaths. On count alone, there is no year-on-year deterioration or improvement. However, with n=3, this numerical equality is likely coincidence.

🚑

Emergency vs In-Hospital Shift

Feb 2025: 2 out of 3 deaths were in the ER (cardiac arrest, AMI) — acute out-of-hospital presentations. Feb 2026: all 3 were admitted inpatients with prolonged stays — complex in-hospital deterioration.

📅

Older Patient Profile

Average age rose from 71.3 to 82.0 years (+10.7 yrs). This may reflect a genuine trend toward older decedents, or simply the random composition of 3-case samples in each year.

💊

Disease Complexity Increased

Feb 2025 diagnoses (sepsis, cardiac arrest, AMI) are common final-pathway conditions. Feb 2026 (empyema, TTP, CA small bowel) are rarer, more complex entities requiring specialist-intensive management.
